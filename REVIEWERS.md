# Editorial Board Structure

## Terminology

- The sole administrative body of GTMC is the Editorial Board.
- Members of the Editorial Board are collectively referred to as "Editors" or "Article Editors."

## Seat Composition

The editorial board operates on a hybrid model that combines a stable core with a rotating exterior.

1. **Permanent Seats**
   - These seats are held by core personnel such as Petris and void.
   - They are responsible for underlying architectural decisions, arbitration of major disputes, and system revisions.
   - Promotions to these seats are currently by invitation only.
   - Note that permanent seats currently only have a promotion path and do not feature a standard exit mechanism.

2. **Rotating Seats**
   - These seats are filled by active editors selected periodically from the candidate pool.
   - These members handle the specific approval processes for all PRs, Submissions, and Features during their active term.
   - Promotions to these seats are by invitation: anyone who has contributed at least one PR may be invited to join the rotation candidate pool. Quantitative conditions may be introduced later if necessary.

## Rotation Mechanism

Each rotation cycle lasts for one month, though this duration can be flexibly adjusted.

- The number of active editors per cycle is tentatively set to three.
- Selection is conducted using a weighted random process.
- Participation is non-mandatory: members can choose whether to participate in the current cycle based on their personal schedules. In principle, participation is not strictly required of every member.
- We adhere to a resting principle to prevent editors from serving consecutive cycles. Members who served in the previous cycle will automatically have their selection priority lowered to the minimum for the current cycle, unless there is an extreme shortage of personnel in the relevant expertise pool.
- We enforce a strict recusal policy where any content involving an active editor must be cross-reviewed by other members of the board.
